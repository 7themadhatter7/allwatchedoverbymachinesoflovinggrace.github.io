The Question

Does geometric pathway encoding preserve learned knowledge better than standard tensor encoding with backpropagation? Train both on identical tasks, then add new material in rounds and measure what each model forgets.

Training Recall: The Core Result

After initial training, new distractor tasks are added in 5 rounds. After each round, we re-test both models on the original training data. Can they still recall what they first learned?

Geometric Model

98.8%
Training recall — constant across all rounds
Pathways printed: 90
All survive: YES
Degradation: 0.0%

Standard Model (500 epochs)

82.3%
Training recall — after 5 distractor rounds
Weight drift: 0.066
All survive: NO
Degradation: -3.7%
Key Finding: The geometric model could not lose knowledge even if we tried. Its pathways are physically separate — incompressible. New learning cannot overwrite old learning. The standard model's shared weights drift with every training round, eroding previously learned patterns. Weight drift is monotonic and irreversible.

Weight Drift: The Blowtorch in Motion

Each training round adjusts the standard model's weights to accommodate new tasks. This adjustment necessarily disturbs the weights that encoded previous tasks. The drift is cumulative and monotonically increasing.

Deeper grooves on individual examples create more interference between examples. The blowtorch burns deeper but also destroys more. The geometric model is unaffected — it doesn't iterate.

Additional Findings

One-Pass vs 500 Epochs

The geometric model achieved 98.8% training recall in a single pass. The standard model achieved only 86.0% after 500 epochs per example. More computation produced a worse result.

Novel Tasks (Never Trained)

Geometric: 69.1% — Standard: 73.0%
Neither model generalizes well to a completely different task family (symmetry). This is expected — the novel family exercises a different trunk entirely.

Verdict

Geometric encoding produces zero degradation across all training rounds.
Standard encoding shows monotonic weight drift and progressive knowledge loss.
